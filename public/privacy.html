1. Who we are & who uses JobTally

JobTally has two kinds of users:

  • Business owners / contractors — paid subscribers who create an account, set up their company, add jobs and clients, and invite workers.
  • Workers / crew — people invited by an owner to clock in and out of jobs. Workers use JobTally for free.

For most data that a business owner enters about their company, jobs, clients, and workers, the business owner is the "controller" of that data and JobTally acts as a service provider / processor handling it on the owner's behalf. For account-level information and for operating the Service overall, JobTally acts as a controller. If you are a worker and have questions about how your data is used within a company's account, you should also contact the business owner who invited you.

2. Information we collect

a. Account information

When you register or are invited, we collect your name, email address, company name (for owners), and a password (stored only in hashed/encrypted form — we never see your plain-text password).

b. Location data

For workers, we capture GPS geolocation at clock-in and clock-out. See Section 3 for full detail. We do not continuously track location in the background between clock-in and clock-out.

c. Job, project & business data (entered by the owner)

  • Job and project details (names, descriptions, addresses, status).
  • Client / customer contact information entered by the owner (such as a customer's name, phone, email, address).
  • Estimates, invoices, and payment amounts.
  • Hourly pay rates and computed labor costs.
  • Time entries and hours worked.
  • Mileage.

d. Photos & uploaded files

Photos of receipts and jobsite photos that you or your workers upload. Receipt images are also processed by our AI provider to extract the store name and amount (see Section 8).

e. Payment information

Subscription billing is handled by Stripe. JobTally does not collect or store full payment card numbers — Stripe does. We receive limited billing metadata such as your subscription status, plan, the last four digits and card brand, and billing history. See Section 7.

f. Technical & usage information

Like most web apps, we automatically receive certain technical data when you use the Service: IP address, browser and device type, operating system, timestamps, pages/screens viewed, and basic diagnostic/error logs. This helps us keep the Service secure and working.

3. Worker location tracking

What is collected: your device's GPS coordinates (latitude/longitude), captured only at the moment you tap "clock in" and the moment you tap "clock out." An approximate accuracy radius and timestamp are stored with each point.

Why: so the business owner can verify that time was recorded at or near the jobsite and can accurately cost labor to a job.

Who sees it: the business owner (and their authorized account users) who invited you. JobTally personnel may access it only as needed to operate or support the Service.

What is not collected: we do not track your location continuously, in the background, or while you are off the clock. Location is a snapshot at clock-in and clock-out only.

Your device or browser controls location permissions. If you deny location access, clock-in/clock-out may be limited or may record without a location point, depending on your employer's settings. The decision to require location-based time tracking is made by your employer, not by JobTally. If you are uncomfortable with location capture, raise it with the business owner who invited you.

4. How we use information

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the Service (accounts, time tracking, job costing, estimates, invoices).
  • Capture and display clock-in/clock-out times and locations to the business owner.
  • Extract store name and amount from receipt photos using AI to speed up expense entry.
  • Process subscription payments and manage billing.
  • Send transactional messages such as invitations, clock-in/clock-out notifications, password resets, and billing notices.
  • Provide customer support and respond to your requests.
  • Monitor, secure, debug, and improve the Service.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use your business's job, client, location, or financial data to serve you third-party advertising.

5. Legal bases for processing

Where data-protection laws such as the GDPR apply, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Contract — to provide the Service you or your employer signed up for (e.g., creating your account, tracking time, billing subscriptions).
  • Legitimate interests — to secure, maintain, and improve the Service and prevent fraud and abuse, balanced against your rights.
  • Consent — where required, for example device-level location permissions, which you grant through your browser/device and can withdraw.
  • Legal obligation — to keep records and comply with tax, accounting, and other laws.

For worker data processed on an owner's behalf, the business owner is responsible for having an appropriate legal basis and for informing workers about location tracking as required by their local laws.

7. Payments & card data

Subscriptions are billed through Stripe, a PCI-DSS-certified payment processor. When you enter payment details, they go directly to Stripe. JobTally never receives or stores your full card number, CVC, or full card details. We store only limited billing metadata (such as subscription status, plan, card brand, last four digits, and billing history) so we can manage your subscription. Stripe's handling of your payment data is governed by Stripe's Privacy Policy.

8. AI receipt scanning

When you upload a receipt photo, we send the image to the Anthropic Claude API to automatically read the store name and amount so you don't have to type them. We use this only to populate your expense entry. The extracted results are shown to you and can be edited. Anthropic processes the image as our subprocessor. AI extraction may occasionally be inaccurate, so you should review scanned values — see the disclaimers in our Terms of Service.

9. Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as your (or your employer's) account is active and as needed to provide the Service. After an account is deleted, we delete or de-identify associated personal data within a commercially reasonable period, except where we must retain certain records to comply with legal, tax, accounting, or dispute-resolution obligations, or to enforce our agreements. Backups are purged on a rolling schedule. Because a business owner controls their workers' and clients' data, some worker or client data may persist in an owner's account until the owner deletes it or their account.

10. Security

  • Encryption in transit and at rest — data is transmitted over HTTPS/TLS and stored encrypted at rest by our infrastructure providers.
  • Row-Level Security (RLS) — our database enforces row-level security policies so that each account can only access its own data; one company cannot see another company's jobs, clients, workers, or files.
  • Hashed passwords — passwords are stored using industry-standard one-way hashing; we cannot read them.
  • Access controls — internal access to production data is limited to personnel who need it to operate and support the Service.

No system is perfectly secure. While we work hard to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. If we become aware of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify you and regulators as required by law.
