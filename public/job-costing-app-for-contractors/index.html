What job costing actually means for a contractor

Job costing is simple in theory: add up everything a job cost you — labor hours, materials, subs, mileage — and compare it to what you charged. What's left is your real profit on that job. The problem is that on a busy crew, those numbers live in three places: hours in a notebook, receipts crumpled in the truck, and the invoice total in your head. By the time you add it all up, the job is long finished and it's too late to fix a job that was bleeding.

JobTally pulls all three into one place, in real time:

Labor

Your crew taps one button to clock in — GPS-stamped so you know they were on the job. Hours roll straight into the job's labor cost.

Materials

Snap a photo of the supply-house receipt. JobTally reads the store and the total; you tap the job it belongs to. Done.

Profit

Every job shows what you're charging, what's gone out, and what's left for you — live, updated as the job runs.
