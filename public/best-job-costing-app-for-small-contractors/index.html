Best job costing app for small contractors (2026)

There's no single "best" app for everyone — it depends on whether you want a focused profit-tracking tool or a full field-service suite. Here's an honest read on JobTally, Jobber, Housecall Pro, and QuickBooks, what each is genuinely good at, and how to pick.

The short version: If you run a 2–10 person crew and mainly want to know the real profit on each job — with GPS clock-in and receipt scanning, at a flat price that doesn't climb per user — JobTally is built for exactly that. Jobber and Housecall Pro are strong all-in-one field-service platforms (scheduling, dispatch, CRM) if you need the whole suite. QuickBooks is the pick when accounting comes first. How to pick — four questions

  • Is job costing included, or locked behind a tier? If profit-per-job is the reason you're buying, make sure it's in the plan you're paying for. In JobTally it's the base product.
  • Does the price climb with each crew member? Per-seat pricing can quietly balloon on a growing crew. JobTally is flat with unlimited crew.
  • How do labor and material costs get in? If it's manual entry, it won't happen. GPS clock-in and receipt scanning (both in JobTally) capture costs without extra work.
  • Do you see profit live or after the fact? Real-time per-job margin lets you fix a bleeding job before it's done — that's the whole point of job costing.
