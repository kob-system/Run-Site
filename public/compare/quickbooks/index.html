JobTally vs QuickBooks

This isn't really either/or. QuickBooks is accounting software; JobTally is a field job-costing tool. Most contractors are best served using both — here's how they differ and where each earns its keep.

Short answer: JobTally shows live per-job profit in the field using GPS crew clock-in and receipt scanning, from your phone — job costing as the work happens. QuickBooks is accounting-first: bookkeeping, taxes, and financial statements, with job/project costing usually on higher tiers and oriented around the books. They're complementary — many contractors run JobTally for day-to-day job profit and QuickBooks for the accounting. Verify QuickBooks' current pricing on their site.

JobTally vs QuickBooks — FAQ

What's the difference?

JobTally is a field job-costing tool showing live per-job profit via GPS clock-in and receipt scanning. QuickBooks is accounting software for bookkeeping and taxes, with job costing usually on higher tiers.

Can JobTally replace QuickBooks?

Not entirely — it's not full accounting. It gives live per-job profit and clean tax exports so your accountant still gets good numbers. Most contractors run both.

Why not just use QuickBooks for job costing?

Accounting-software job costing usually means after-the-fact entry and a higher tier. JobTally captures costs live and shows projected profit before the job's finished.
