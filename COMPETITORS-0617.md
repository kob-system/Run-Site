## Positioning brief (synthesized)

Run-Site Positioning Brief

"See your profit on every job — no per-tech tax, no surprise bill, works in the basement." Run-Site is the only tool in this cluster that does live per-job profit math (materials + labor + profit → contract price) on a phone-first, offline-first app — for one flat price with every worker included. Everyone else sells quoting, scheduling, or dispatch. Nobody else tells the owner whether the job actually made money.

1. Live per-job profit + AI receipt scan. The thing none of them do. Jobber, Housecall Pro, Workiz, FieldPulse, Joist, JobNimbus all stop at quote/invoice. 2. Flat price, every worker included — no per-seat penalty. The #1 churn driver across the field is per-user/per-tech billing (Jobber +$29/user, ServiceTitan $250-500/tech, JobNimbus $20-75/user, Workiz 5-seat minimum). Run-Site doesn't punish you for hiring. One build + one flat recurring.

Run-Site keeps working with no signal and syncs clean. Built for the phone in the truck, not a desktop dispatch board. Bonus trust hook: one-click self-serve cancel (Stripe) and you own your data — the direct opposite of HCP's cancellation traps and ServiceTitan's $24K-$46K contract jail.

THE PRICE STORY (vs rivals)
Run-Site = one-time build + small flat recurring, all workers included, no add-on paywalls, processing fees kept under Joist's 3.49%+$0.49. Frame it against each rival's real loaded cost, not their advertised teaser:

## Competitor profiles

### Jobber
**Who it's for:** Solo operators and small residential home-service teams (under ~10) in HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, cleaning, electrical. The category-default 'easiest' app, mobile-first, leans office/quoting workflow more than field-labor tracking.

**Pricing:** 2026 tiers (monthly, no-commitment): Solo Core $39/mo (1 user), Connect $119, Grow $199. Team: Connect $169/mo (5 users), Grow $349/mo (10 users), Plus $599/mo (15 users). Every extra user = +$29/mo. Add-ons bill monthly only (no annual discount): AI Receptionist $99/mo, Marketing Suite $79/mo (free only on Plus), standalone Reviews $39 / Campaigns $29 / Referrals $29. Payment processing 2.9%+$0.30 card, 2.7%+$0.30 tap, 1% ACH, +1% instant payout. Annual prepay saves up to ~35%. 14-day trial, no card. A 'realistic' loaded stack (CompanyCam, measurement, phone, etc.) runs ~$1,362/mo before processing; a 10-person team commonly lands $450-750/mo once add-ons and overage users stack.

**Strengths:**
  - Best-in-class ease of use — repeatedly called 'the easiest software I've ever used'
  - Excellent mobile apps (iOS 4.8/5, Android 4.7/5) — a genuinely high bar
  - Client Hub self-service booking/payments and automated reminders that 'save hours every week'
  - Strong brand trust / category default (200K+ users), responsive support
  - Polished quoting -> job -> invoice flow

**Weaknesses (real complaints):**
  - Per-user pricing punishes growth: +$29/user/mo means crews quietly push bills past $600/mo; competitors market '75% savings / $3,709+/yr saved' vs loaded Jobber and contractors are actively switching to escape it
  - Add-ons can't be prepaid annually and the high-value ones (AI Receptionist $99, Marketing Suite $79) are paywalled off lower tiers — nickel-and-diming
  - QuickBooks sync is the #1 functional complaint: ~2% of line items drop during sync, frequent breakdowns needing manual reconciliation
  - Weak/locked reporting (~5/10): can't build custom reports, no predictive analytics
  - Dispatching is manual drag-and-drop only; no AI/auto technician assignment
  - Offline mode is partial and inconsistent: can't create jobs or take payments offline, conflicting reports on what actually syncs; 2025 reviews cite crashes, slowness, and 'won't connect' even on 5G
  - No real job-level profit/cost tracking (materials/labor/profit per job) — it's a quoting/scheduling tool, not a margin tool

**↳ Run-Site's wedge:** Run-Site can win on flat, predictable pricing with no per-user tax (Jobber's biggest churn driver), genuine offline-first sync for field crews (Jobber's weakest spot), and the thing Jobber simply doesn't do: live per-job profit (materials/labor/profit -> contract price) plus AI receipt scan. Position Run-Site as 'one build + simple recurring, every worker included, see your margin on every job' against Jobber's creeping per-seat + add-on bill.

### Housecall Pro
**Who it's for:** Established small-to-mid home-service businesses (~5-20 employees) on iOS with flexible budgets that prioritize scheduling/invoicing/online booking. Explicitly NOT recommended for solo operators, tight-budget startups, or anyone needing real-time GPS/route optimization.

**Pricing:** 2026 tiers: Basic $79/mo ($59 annual) 1 user; Essentials $189/mo ($149 annual) up to 5 users; MAX from ~$299-329/mo (custom, unlimited users). Add-ons stack fast: Sales Proposals $40/mo, Vehicle GPS $20/vehicle/mo, Recurring Service Plans $40/mo, Price Book $149/mo. Payment processing fees on top (users report them 'higher than expected'). No contract, cancel anytime (in theory — see weaknesses). Capterra 4.7 (2,736 reviews), G2 4.3 (200). 14-day trial.

**Strengths:**
  - Intuitive drag-and-drop scheduling and professional invoicing — 'scheduling and invoicing just work without drama'
  - Strong iOS app (4.5/5) and effective online booking
  - Fast estimate-to-job conversion
  - Large trusted brand with deep review volume and broad HVAC/plumbing/electrical adoption

**Weaknesses (real complaints):**
  - Billing & cancellation disputes are the highest-volume complaint: users charged for months after confirmed cancellation (one documented $1,521 over 9 months); no self-service cancel button, forced through chat/email/phone that 'often fail'
  - Aggressive cost creep / add-on trap — the #1 reason users leave per Capterra: sign up at $59-79, then core features (QuickBooks sync, GPS, estimate builder) force an upgrade to Essentials plus paid add-ons; a 5-tech shop realistically hits $1,600-1,700/mo (~27x the advertised rate)
  - Android app is weak (3.3/5) — slow, 'undercooked,' minimal offline support; techs can't edit jobs without connectivity
  - Phone support locked to the top MAX tier (~$329/mo); lower tiers get AI-gated chat only — 'no support unless you want to speak with AI'
  - GPS updates only every few minutes (not real-time), no built-in route optimization, no geofencing, manual invoice line-item entry, no automatic payroll calc
  - Reporting/customization runs out at scale; advanced analytics/inventory need third-party tools

**↳ Run-Site's wedge:** Run-Site's strongest wedge here is trust + transparency: a frictionless self-serve cancel (Stripe), honest flat pricing with workers included instead of the add-on trap, and a field-grade offline-first mobile experience (HCP's Android is its Achilles heel). HCP also has no true job-profit engine, weak real-time GPS, and locks support behind $329/mo — Run-Site can offer GPS clock-in/out, live profit per job, AI receipt scan, and founder-level support without paywalling it. Pitch: 'phone-first for the crew, owner sees real profit, no surprise bill, cancel in one click.'

Add-ons (Marketing Pro, Phones Pro, Pricebook Pro) are extra custom quotes. One-time implementation $5,000-$50,000+. Mandatory 12-month minimum contract with auto-renewal; no month-to-month. Real-world 10-tech HVAC team: ~$63K year one, ~$48-54K/yr after. Renewal price hikes of 15-20% reported.

  - Strong brand/credibility (public company); 'the system you grow into' narrative resonates with ambitious owners
  - Robust integrations ecosystem and add-on modules for nearly every workflow

**Weaknesses (real complaints):**
  - PRICE SHOCK is the #1 exit driver: $250-500/tech/mo plus $5K-$50K implementation. Small shops describe paying $1,000+/mo and 'barely using' what they pay for. BBB rating ~1.3/5 with 30+ complaints

  - Overwhelming complexity for small teams: 'It's almost like it's too big to where my people are scared to dive in.' 'The product is complicated, which means you need help.' Most features go unused by sub-20-tech shops

(3) YOU OWN YOUR DATA (owned Supabase stack) -- the opposite of ST's export-proof lock-in. ### Spreadsheets (Excel / Google Sheets, often a free template)
**Who it's for:** The default for the true bottom of the market: 1-3 person trades shops (HVAC, electrical, handyman, remodel) who track jobs, budgets, and 'did I make money' in a homemade workbook because it's free and they already know it.

**Pricing:** Free (Google Sheets) or bundled with Office/M365 (~$7-10/mo). Job-costing templates sold for ~$20-50 one-time on Gumroad/Workspace Marketplace.

**Strengths:**
  - Zero or near-zero cost and no vendor lock-in
  - Infinitely flexible - the contractor models his exact budget math (materials/labor/profit -> price)
  - No learning curve they don't already have; no onboarding, no login friction
  - Works offline and on any device they own

**Weaknesses (real complaints):**
  - Version-control collapse is the FIRST thing to break: files get copied, emailed, edited on different devices, formulas drift, and nobody trusts which file is current (per IntelTech analysis)
  - Data lags reality - field hours and receipts arrive after the job is done, so cost problems are discovered weeks too late when course-correction options are gone
  - No live labor capture: a desktop sheet cannot clock a worker in/out with GPS, so labor cost (the biggest variable) is guessed or back-filled
  - Receipts pile up in a shoebox/truck console; manual re-keying is error-prone and time-consuming
  - Construction pros reportedly lose ~35% of time on non-optimal work (searching for data, resolving conflicts, fixing mistakes) - spreadsheets amplify this
  - Not phone-first: editing a formula-heavy workbook on a phone in the field is painful
  - No multi-user roles - the owner can't safely give a worker limited access without exposing the whole sheet

**↳ Run-Site's wedge:** This is Run-Site's single best wedge. The spreadsheet user ALREADY thinks in materials/labor/profit -> contract price - that is literally Run-Site's job-creation flow, so there's no concept to teach, just a better container. Run-Site replaces the workbook with one shared source of truth that updates live: workers clock in/out with GPS so labor cost is real (not guessed), AI receipt scan kills the shoebox and manual re-keying, and profit-per-job updates DURING the job instead of at month-end when it's too late to fix. Pitch: 'Keep your exact budget math, lose the version chaos and the lag.' Phone-first beats a desktop sheet in a truck. Price it just above free so the only switching cost is a few dollars vs. hours of reconciliation.

### QuickBooks Online (Simple Start / Plus / Advanced)
**Who it's for:** Contractors who 'graduated' to real accounting, or whose bookkeeper/accountant told them to use it. Plus is positioned as the contractor tier (project profitability + 1099 management). The aspirational/respectable choice that small shops over-buy.

**Pricing:** 2026 list: Solopreneur $20/mo, Simple Start $38/mo, Essentials $75/mo, Plus $115/mo (the contractor/project-profitability tier), Advanced $275/mo. Frequent 50%-off-3-months promos that then jump to full price - a known resentment trigger.

**Strengths:**
  - Industry-standard accounting; accountants and lenders expect it
  - Plus has genuine project profitability, budgets, class/location tracking, 1099 contractor management
  - Deep ecosystem and integrations; handles taxes/payroll/banking end to end
  - Trusted brand - low perceived risk for the owner

**Weaknesses (real complaints):**
  - Widely described as 'a lot more complicated than it needs to be' - powerful but overkill for a one-man shop on cash accounting (forum sentiment)
  - Job costing is notoriously hard to set up correctly; contractors openly admit 'I don't have job costing set up right so I don't use it' - they pay for the feature and never get value
  - Real project profitability is locked behind the $115/mo Plus tier - expensive for a solo contractor who just wants 'did this job make money'
  - Aggressive, recurring price hikes and constant upsell prompts - a top complaint driving people to look for alternatives (NerdWallet)
  - Not field-/phone-first: it's a back-office accounting tool, not something a worker uses on a roof. No native worker time clock with GPS tied to job cost
  - Steep learning curve; many need to hire a bookkeeper to run it, adding hidden cost

**↳ Run-Site's wedge:** Run-Site should NOT try to be QuickBooks - it should be the operational layer QB is bad at and sit alongside it. Two attack lines: (1) Cost/complexity - a solo contractor pays $115/mo for Plus mostly for project profitability they can't configure; Run-Site delivers live profit-per-job out of the box with no accountant required, for far less. **Weaknesses (real complaints):**

Positioning: 'Free invoicing apps tell you who owes you. They don't tell you if the job was worth doing. Run-Site is built for the trades - it costs the job, tracks the crew, scans the receipts, and shows your real profit while the job is still open.' Beat Wave's receipt-scan paywall by including AI receipt scan as a core feature, not an add-on.
